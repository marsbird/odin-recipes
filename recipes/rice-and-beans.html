Rice and Beans

Description

Now this is a dish that I can honestly say I've loved all my life. Actually, it would be more honest to say that I can't remember a time in my life when I didn't love a simple bowl of rice and beans, cuban style.

It must be a result of growing up in on the Atlantic coast of south Florida, because even though the way the Puerto Ricans make rice and beans is in my genes, the way the Cubans do it is in my heart.

Cook the beans in sofrito, sazon goya and bacon fat, let it simmer until all the spices and flavors have mixed, and the moment you life the lid and the rushing steam heats your cheeks, you'll know that you'll never go back.

Ingredients

  • black beans
  • sofrito cooking base
  • vegetable oil
  • bacon (or bacon fat)
  • white rice
  • sazon goya
  • chicken bouillon
  • sugar
  • red wine vinegar

Steps

  1. Add one part rice and one and one half parts water to pot, with salt and vegetable oil
  2. Heat on high until boiling, then turn temperature to low and continue to cook under pot lid
  3. Fry bacon in pan over low heat to release fat
  4. Add sofrito, sazon, chicken bouillon and a splash of water to pan and cook down
  5. Turn heat to medium and add beans to pan
  6. Add equal parts sugar and red wine vinegar (only a pinch/splash of each)
  7. Cook until most of the liquid has evaporated and serve over white rice
